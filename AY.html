Arthur Yip

Bio

Arthur Yip analyses and simulates transportation and energy systems at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) as a Postdoctoral Researcher. He focuses on modelling consumer choice, engineering design, and government policy decisions in transportation energy and automotive markets. His research typically fits in the emergent field of macro-energy systems.

Education

Carnegie Mellon University | Pittsburgh, PA

Ph.D. in Engineering & Public Policy | 2015 - 2020

Massachusetts Institute of Technology | Cambridge, MA

Sc.M. in Technology & Policy (Engineering Systems) | 2012 - 2014

University of Waterloo | Waterloo, ON

B.A.Sc. in Chemical Engineering (Co-op) | 2007 - 2012

Experience

National Renewable Energy Laboratory | Postdoctoral Researcher | 2020 - present

Breakthrough Institute | Energy Systems Engineer | 2014 - 2015
